## EXECUTIVE ROTATION PHILOSOPHY

The Las Vegas Aces possess a **championship-caliber core trio** (A'ja Wilson +29.3 net, Jackie Young +22.5 net, Chelsea Gray +13.2 net) that combines for **+64.9 net rating differential**. The team's success depends on maximizing elite talent minutes while strategically deploying role players to complement strengths and minimize weaknesses.

**Core Principle:** Championship windows are finite. With Wilson (age 29, year 8) and Gray (age 31, year 11) in prime/late-prime years, the priority is winning now by optimizing their impact through intelligent lineup construction.

- Strategy: Elite core trio (+64.9 net) + paint presence (Smith) + spacing (Bell)

- A'ja Wilson, Jackie Young, Chelsea Gray, Jewell Loyd, NaLyssa Smith

**NEVER in Final 6 Minutes:**
- Dana Evans (negative impact too severe)

- Evans + Nye together (combined -23.0 net = disaster)

### BLOWOUT WINS (Leading by 15+)

**Lineup Priority: Rest Starters, Develop Bench**
- NaLyssa Smith, Jewell Loyd, Kierstan Bell, Dana Evans, Aaliyah Nye
- Expected Net: -2.8 average (acceptable for bench unit)
- Strategy: Smith anchors (+8.0 net), rest elite trio for next game
- Substitution: If lead shrinks to <10, insert at least one elite player

**Rest Strategy:**
- A'ja Wilson: Sit entire 4th quarter if ahead by 20+ with 10:00 remaining
- Jackie Young: Sit at 8:00 mark in 4th if ahead by 18+
- Chelsea Gray: Sit at 6:00 mark in 4th if ahead by 15+
- Goal: Preserve energy for playoffs, avoid injury risk

---

### BLOWOUT LOSSES (Trailing by 18+)

**Lineup Priority: Development + Concede**
- NaLyssa Smith, Jewell Loyd, Kierstan Bell, Dana Evans, Aaliyah Nye
- Strategy: Give young players experience, save elite trio for next game
- Mental approach: Focus on individual skill development, not outcome

**When to Concede:**
- 4th quarter, trailing by 18+ with <8:00 remaining
- Starters played 30+ MPG already (rest priority)
- Back-to-back games (preserve energy for tomorrow)
- Do NOT: Continue playing starters 36+ minutes in blowout loss

---

### OPPONENT ON RUN (8-0 or Larger Spurt)

**Immediate Response: Call Timeout + Insert Offense**
- Lineup: Wilson, Young, Gray, Loyd, Smith (Offensive Firepower)
- Strategy: Match opponent's intensity with elite scoring punch
- Alternative: Small Ball Blitz if pace change needed (Wilson at 5)
- Goal: Stop bleeding within 2 minutes or substitute again

**If Run Continues (12-0 or Worse):**
- Insert Defensive Lockdown elements (Wilson, Young, Bell, Smith, Loyd)
- Strategy: Slow pace, force tough shots, limit possessions
- Message: Defense creates offense - get stops first

---

### FOUL TROUBLE ADJUSTMENTS

**A'ja Wilson with 3+ Fouls (Before 4th Quarter):**
- Immediate sit: Cannot risk 4th foul
- Replacement: NaLyssa Smith plays extended minutes (26-28 MPG)
- Offensive adjustment: Gray-Young pick-and-roll becomes primary
- Return: 4th quarter at 10:00 mark (maximize remaining impact)

**Jackie Young with 3+ Fouls:**
- Immediate sit: Too valuable to risk
- Replacement: Jewell Loyd enters for defensive continuity
- Offensive adjustment: Gray usage increases, Loyd spot-up role
- Return: Depends on foul timing (ideally final 8:00)

**Chelsea Gray with 4+ Fouls:**
- Conditional sit: If turnover count also high (4+ TOs), sit immediately
- Replacement: Jackie Young becomes primary ball-handler
- Offensive adjustment: Young runs point (85th percentile AST% capable)
- Return: Final 4:00 if game close and only 4 fouls

**Multiple Elite in Foul Trouble (2+ with 3+ Fouls):**
- Strategy: Stagger returns - never sit both A'ja + Jackie simultaneously
- Priority order: A'ja > Jackie > Chelsea (based on net rating impact)
- Offensive adjustment: Simplify to pick-and-roll with whoever's available

## OPPONENT-SPECIFIC ROTATION STRATEGIES

### vs. ELITE PERIMETER OFFENSE (Seattle Storm, Dallas Wings)

**Defensive Priority: Limit Three-Point Shooting**
- Starting Lineup: Wilson, Young, Gray, Bell, Smith (defensive versatility)
- Jackie Young Assignment: Opponent's best perimeter scorer
- Bell/Loyd: Switch on screens, stay attached to shooters
- Smith + Wilson: Protect paint but closeout on drive-and-kicks
- Rotation: Defensive Lockdown lineup gets extended minutes (16-18 MPG)

**Offensive Counter:**
- Possess the ball: Slow pace, milk clock (reduce opponent possessions)
- Gray pick-and-roll: Create 2-on-1 advantages, force help, kick to open shooter
- Smith offensive glass: Create second chances (opponent in transition less)

---

### vs. SIZE-HEAVY INTERIOR TEAMS (Minnesota Lynx, New York Liberty)

**Offensive Priority: Dominate the Paint**
- Starting Lineup: Wilson, Young, Gray, Bell, Smith (twin towers)
- Post-ups: Feed Wilson early and often (100th percentile AST% for C = also facilitates)
- Smith Usage: Increase to 24-26 MPG for paint presence
- Gray: High-low passes between Wilson and Smith
- Rotation: Twin towers together 20-22 MPG (vs. typical 18-20)

**Defensive Counter:**
- Rim protection: Wilson + Smith = elite shot-blocking duo
- Rebounding: Control defensive glass (Wilson 93rd TREB%, Smith 61st TREB%)
- Transition defense: Limit fast breaks (opponent's size advantage in half-court)

---

### vs. SMALL BALL TEAMS (Connecticut Sun, Phoenix Mercury)

**Offensive Priority: Exploit Speed Advantage**
- Starting Lineup: Wilson at 5, Young, Gray, Bell, Loyd (Small Ball Blitz)
- Pace: PUSH RELENTLESSLY - fast break every possession
- Wilson: Operates at elbow/high post, too quick for opponent's bigs
- Gray: Runs pick-and-roll with Wilson at 5 (unstoppable combo)
- Rotation: Small Ball Blitz extended to 22-24 MPG

**Defensive Counter:**
- Switching: All five can switch 1-5 in small-ball scheme
- Perimeter pressure: Deny ball to shooters, force tough twos
- Wilson alone in paint: Acceptable trade-off for speed advantage

---

### vs. WEAK INTERIOR DEFENSE (Bottom-10 Paint Defense)

**Offensive Priority: Score 60+ in Paint**
- Starting Lineup: Wilson, Young, Gray, Bell, Smith (twin towers)
- Feed the post: Wilson + Smith get 40+ combined FGA
- Offensive rebounding: Smith crashes glass aggressively
- Gray: Post entry passes, not perimeter creation
- Rotation: Interior-focused approach entire game

**Strategy:**
- Accept lower pace (more half-court sets)
- High-low game between Wilson and Smith
- Kick-outs to Bell only when defense collapses

**Factor 4: Avoid Negative Pairings**
- NEVER: Evans + Nye together (combined -23.0 net)
- NEVER: Bell + Nye together (spacing illusion)
- NEVER: Gray + Evans together (turnover cascade)
- Discipline: Coaches must resist desperation moves that violate these rules

**Factor 5: Lineup Continuity**
- Championship Closing lineup needs 200+ minutes together in regular season
- Practice time: Run specific sets with exact lineups (not just starters)
- Chemistry: Elite trio together 26-28 MPG builds playoff trust
