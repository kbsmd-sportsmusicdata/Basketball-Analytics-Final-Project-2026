### 3. The Anti-Analytics Champions

The Aces succeed by **violating modern basketball orthodoxy**:

- **Shot Selection Rank:** #13 in WNBA in Rim+3PT frequency (53% "good" shots)
- **Efficiency Rank:** #4 in WNBA in True Shooting % (55.2%)

**Translation:** They take the 13th-most "analytics-friendly" shots but achieve the 4th-best efficiency. They win by making "bad" (midrange) shots at elite rates, particularly through A'ja Wilson's 71.5% midrange shot frequency.

**Strategic Advantage:** This profile creates a **mismatch problem** for opponents who scheme for modern offenses. The Aces can:
1. Exploit defensive attention on the 3-point line through elite midrange efficiency
2. Leverage A'ja's dominant post game when defenses collapse
3. Attack second-chance opportunities (rebounding strength)

## CRITICAL PRIORITIES FOR COACHING STAFF

### Priority 1: Maximize Core 3 Minutes Together
**Target:** 28-30 minutes per game for Wilson-Young-Gray trio

The +64.9 combined net impact represents a **20+ point advantage per 48 minutes** when all three play. Stagger rest sparingly and only when ahead by 10+.

### Priority 2: Solve Chelsea Gray's Turnover Problem
**Current:** 16th percentile TOV% (weakness)  
**Target:** 50th percentile or better

Gray's elite playmaking (89th percentile AST%) is undermined by turnovers. Focus areas:
- Decision-making in transition (slow down)
- Pick-and-roll reads (earlier passes)
- Limit high-risk cross-court passes

### Priority 3: Develop Kierstan Bell's Shot Conversion
**Current:** 93rd percentile 3PT attempt rate, 3rd percentile TS%  
**Issue:** Right shot profile, poor execution

Bell provides elite spacing threat but doesn't convert. Development focus:
- Shooting mechanics refinement
- Shot selection within 3PT attempts (corner vs above break)
- Confidence-building in practice reps

### Priority 4: Manage Aaliyah Nye's Development
**Current:** 98th percentile 3PT rate, 10th percentile TS%, -10.7 net impact  
**Approach:** Limited situational minutes

Nye is a pure specialist still developing. Use only:
- Garbage time for experience
- Specific matchups requiring shooting spacing
- Never with Dana Evans (both negative impact)

### Priority 5: Maintain Phase 2 Shooting Excellence
**Key Risk:** Regression to Phase 1 shooting (47.8% eFG%)  
**Safeguard:** Monitor individual TS% weekly, rhythm maintenance

The championship window depends on sustaining 55%+ eFG%. Weekly tracking of:
- Individual shot charts (hot/cold zones)
- Assisted vs unassisted rates
- Shot quality metrics (expected vs actual FG%)
